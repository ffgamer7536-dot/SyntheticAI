Algorithmic Enhancements: Hard Example Mining

To combat the immense difficulty of mapping rare, minute classes such as Ground Clutter, Logs, and Flowers in chaotic off-road environments, we evolved our DeepLabV3+ architecture using an automated Hard Example Mining (HEM) pipeline.

  • Dynamic Failure Isolation: Our pipeline performs baseline evaluation sweeps across our initial data distributions, selectively sequestering bounding indices and paths for the bottom 30% most difficult terrain instances into an auxiliary dataloader.
  • Punishing Augmentations: These mined instances natively bypass standard augmentation, triggering an aggressive get_hard_transforms() matrix that inflicts heavy coarse dropouts, aggressive saturation jittering, noise mapping, and destructive geometric scaling.
  • Automated Rebalancing: The core DataLoader hooks into failure metadata to multiply representational weights iteratively up to 4x, artificially compounding the likelihood of training directly on the weakest environmental spots without catastrophic dataset duplication.

This automated exposure mechanism directly catalyzed our massive rare class IoU breakthroughs (e.g. from 0 IoU mapping on Flowers/Logs up to reliable 0.25-0.38 IoU hits) by forcing the neural network to overcome its foundational terrain blindspots.
